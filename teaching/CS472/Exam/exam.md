## Final Exam Information

The final exam will consist of:

- **20 multiple choice questions**
- **4 short essay answer questions**

---

### Format and Schedule

- The exam will be **online** and available for **24 hours on May 12th**.
- You may take the exam at any time within that 24-hour window.

#### Optional In-Person Alternative

If you prefer to take the exam **in person, written on paper**, I can accommodate that.

**Please inform me in advance** if you plan to take the in-person exam so that I can prepare and print the necessary materials.

The in-person exam will be offered during the officially scheduled exam time on MyUNLV:

- **Date:** May 12th
- **Time:** 6:00 PM – 8:00 PM
- **Location:** CHB A108

---

### What to Study

All questions will be based on:

- **Lecture slides**, including the GenAI material
- **Labs**
- **Team project experience**

Focus areas include:

- Collaborative software development practices
- Version control and GitHub workflows
- Pull requests, code review, and branching strategies
- Basic testing strategies
- Continuous Integration (CI) concepts introduced in labs
- Project management, including issue tracking, milestones, and team coordination
- Use of Generative AI in software development workflows, as discussed in class
- Reasoning, traceability, and justification in pull requests

---

### Preparation Guidance

You **do not need to study outside materials**.

If you have:

- **completed the labs**,
- **reviewed the lecture materials**, and
- **actively contributed to your team project**,

you should be well-prepared for the exam.

The exam is **not intended to be complicated**. It is designed to reflect the work you have already been doing throughout the semester. You can expect **practical questions based on how software projects are collaboratively developed**, including workflows, decision-making, and practices you have applied in your team.

If you have been engaged in the project and course activities, you should find the exam straightforward.
